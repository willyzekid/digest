Emerging themes

1. Measurement transparency is overtaking media access as retail's contested layer

Albertsons' research showing 6.5x variance in reported iROAS ("Albertsons Media Collective unveils Onsite Incrementality Measurement after 6.5x iROAS variance findings") and Criteo's SPARK Matrix recognition for unified measurement ("Criteo named Leader in QKS SPARK Matrix for retail media networks Q2 2026") together signal that retail media has matured past the land-grab phase. Brands no longer ask who has inventory; they demand to see methodology. Coop Norway's nationwide buildout and Raley's 208-store launch confirm that inventory is becoming abundant, which only raises the premium on credible measurement. Expect the second half of 2026 to be defined by methodology disclosures, shadow measurement and third-party audits.

2. Loyalty is being re-platformed around households and cross-portfolio currencies

United's expansion of pooled miles to 40+ partners ("United expands MileagePlus miles pooling to 40+ partner airlines") and Fanatics' integration of FanCash into its prediction market ("Fanatics extends FanCash rewards to its prediction-market trading platform") reveal a structural shift away from individual, in-category currency toward shared, portfolio-wide value. The Uber One and Expedia integration extends the pattern through a subscription super-app wrapper. The unit of loyalty is moving from "a member" toward "a household, on a journey, across a portfolio." Programmes designed around solo-individual mechanics will under-monetise by 2027.

3. Data infrastructure is the prerequisite that finally separates leaders from laggards

Winn-Dixie's replatforming onto Eagle Eye AIR ("Winn-Dixie transitions to unified digital loyalty programme on Eagle Eye AIR platform"), the Visa-Braze commentary ("Visa and Braze argue AI-orchestrated, real-time loyalty is now 'table stakes'") and the UK DUAA complaints regime ("UK Data (Use and Access) Act complaints rights take effect 19 June 2026") all point to one conclusion: AI personalisation, retail-media monetisation and regulatory compliance are three views of the same underlying capability — unified, governed, real-time customer data. Firms that have invested in identity resolution and consent infrastructure can move fast; those that have not are stuck in pilots. The window to close that gap in 2026 is narrowing.

Reflection questions

  1. As retail-media measurement standards converge on incrementality, what proportion of our current personalisation and media investment would still pass a rigorous, third-party audited iROAS test — and how would the answer reshape our 2027 plan?
  2. If loyalty currency is migrating from the individual to the household, portfolio or super-app, where are we still designing our programme around an outdated atomic unit, and what would we have to change about our economics, data model and partner strategy to compete on household lifetime value?
  3. Given that AI-orchestrated personalisation is now table stakes while the differentiator is contextual relevance, what would it take for our organisation to operate a true closed-loop, real-time decisioning capability across CRM, loyalty and channels — and is the bottleneck data, talent or organisational design?
