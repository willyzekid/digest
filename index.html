Emerging themes

The CDP is becoming an agent runtime, not a marketer's database

Adobe's CX Enterprise Coworker launch and the Databricks–Adobe zero-copy integration both reframe the customer-data layer as something an autonomous agent reads in real time, with privileges, audit logs and decisioning rules. SAP and Google Cloud's deeper partnership repeats the pattern from a different starting point: enterprise systems of record feeding multi-agent execution. The implication is that loyalty teams are buying a runtime, not a database. Procurement criteria — observability, agent-level governance, latency under decision load — start to look more like SRE than martech.

Retail media's measurement reset is coming this year, not next

Albertsons' incrementality launch and PayPal's transaction-graph attribution set up the first serious benchmarks for the retail-media spend that has flooded into Walmart, Sam's Club and similar networks. Sam's Club's Retail Experience Network simultaneously pushes the addressable surface beyond screens to fuel pumps and dwell-time formats. The combination forces a reckoning: brands that have been trading "any retailer-reported ROAS" for trade dollars are about to face hard, methodology-aware comparisons. Expect the Q4 2026 trade-marketing planning cycle to be the first where incremental ROAS is the default unit of negotiation.

Regulators have moved on from breach drama to retention and transparency

Italy's €6.6m postal-financial fine and the EDPB's transparency CEA both target practices that loyalty programs and CDPs are structurally prone to: keeping data past its purpose, and explaining it badly. United's two-track MileagePlus and Marriott's expanded partner experiences add to the data-flow complexity that the regulators are now scrutinising. Programs that haven't refreshed retention design and recipient disclosures since the early GDPR years are the most exposed. The risk is not headline fines so much as accumulated, multi-jurisdiction enforcement that grinds operations more than headlines.

Reflection questions

  1. If your CDP became an agent runtime tomorrow, which three governance gaps would your security and legal teams flag first — and is anyone funding the fix today?
  2. How much of your retail-media spend would survive a switch to incremental ROAS measured by an external graph, and which categories would you cut first?
  3. Where in your loyalty program are you accumulating data debt — long retention, vague consent, untested recipient lists — that an EDPB-aligned regulator could surface in a single audit?

Emerging themes

Reverse logistics is splitting into a B2B operations product and a B2C lifestyle product

TurnHome and ResaleDoorstep both ride DHL's January 2026 framing of reverse logistics as a $62.5bn "invisible value pool", but they target opposite ends. TurnHome turns the consumer's closet into a supply line; ResaleDoorstep helps retailers skip the warehouse return-loop entirely. The category will fragment along that axis: separate stacks, different unit economics, different distribution. Brands that wait for a single platform play to emerge will miss the window on both sides.

B2B BNPL and embedded finance are arriving in Europe's mid-market

WorkingCap, PolicyPilot and GreenLedger all assume the same shift: financial products will increasingly be distributed through the workflows their customers already live in, not through standalone fintech apps. Comfi's $65m Pre-Series A in MENA is the proof point that B2B BNPL is institutionally fundable, and Simply Business's AI Advisor award shows AI-mediated insurance buying has crossed the trust threshold. European challenger banks now compete on what they embed, not what they originate. The opening for founders is in productising distribution surfaces, not balance sheets.

Vertical AI agents are eating the long tail of professional services

CallStandIn, LedgerMate, Hospitality Forecast and PolicyPilot each take a single trade or profession and build an agent narrow enough to actually replace human friction — sole-trader accounting, restaurant forecasting, trades-receptionist work, freelancer insurance brokering. The category will fragment along vertical depth rather than horizontal capability, because the unlock is operator UX rather than model quality. Expect winners to look more like vertical communities than software companies, with AI as the silent operations layer underneath.
