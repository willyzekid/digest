Emerging themes

Agent governance is the new CDP buying criterion

Tealium's MCP Configuration Agent, ServiceNow's AI Control Tower, NVIDIA-ServiceNow Project Arc, Cognizant Secure AI Services and the Five Eyes cyber guidance all landed within seven days. Read together, they describe a market where the question is no longer "do you have a CDP" but "how does your CDP behave when an agent misbehaves". Loyalty operators are about to be asked for an agent inventory by name, by permission scope, and by recovery procedure. The vendors that ship that telemetry natively will outsell vendors that bolt it on.

Devaluation has migrated into the issuer-side mechanics

World of Hyatt's chart redesign and Chase Points Boost's quiet hotel-value reshuffle show that the work of devaluation has split between the loyalty program and its co-brand or transfer partners. Members increasingly experience devaluation through a third party they cannot complain to, and which the program itself does not control. That shifts the trust burden from "is the program honest" to "is everyone in the chain honest", and creates a real brand risk for any program whose comms strategy stops at its own boundary.

Subscription-loyalty is now decisively outpacing points-loyalty

Walmart's 15.5% membership-fee revenue growth, Tesco's 76% Clubcard Challenges conversion, and the broader retail-media build-out at Albertsons and small-format chains all point to the same model: paid or commitment-based participation now dominates loyalty unit economics. Points programs without a subscription overlay or supplier-partner monetisation engine will look structurally underperforming inside two years. The action item is not "join the trend" — it is "name the version of this you can credibly run".

Reflection questions

  1. If your security team asked tomorrow for a complete inventory of every AI agent and MCP server connected to your customer data, could you produce one in less than a week?
  2. How much of your loyalty program's perceived devaluation in the last 12 months actually originated outside your direct control — and how would you communicate that to members without losing trust?
  3. What is the smallest possible subscription overlay you could pilot on top of your existing program in 2026, and what would it have to deliver to be ARPU-accretive?

Emerging themes

The competitive frontier has moved from capability to governance

Anthropic's Opus 4.7 release and OpenAI's gated GPT-5.5-Cyber show that the absolute capability frontier is bifurcating, while ServiceNow's AI Control Tower, NVIDIA-ServiceNow Project Arc, Cognizant Secure AI Services and the Five Eyes guidance all describe the same observation: the buyer is no longer asking "how capable is your model" but "how do I prove what your model did". Vendors and buyers that internalise this shift first will define the 2027 procurement playbook. Those that keep selling on benchmark deltas will find themselves outflanked by quieter, governance-led competitors.

The agent-on default has flipped — three big platforms enabled it on the same day

Microsoft Agent 365, Salesforce, Cloudflare and AWS AgentCore Payments all activated autonomous capabilities on 2 May. The simultaneity is significant: it is no longer credible to plan for an "agents off by default" enterprise. Loyalty, CRM, security and finance teams now share a single planning horizon and the question is which workflows are agent-permissible, not which ones could be. The procurement, policy and treasury work needed to make this safe has not kept pace, which is the operational risk of the year.

Investment intensity and physical deployment are converging

Google's $40bn Anthropic commitment, BCC's $650bn-annual investment baseline, AMD's $120bn server-CPU 2030 TAM, and Aurora Innovation's commercial driverless freight all describe a single underlying motion: AI capital is being converted into physical and operational deployment at unprecedented pace. Compute, agents and physical automation are now being financed as a single bet. Strategy teams that still treat them as separate capital lines will mis-price the next planning cycle.

Vertical AI agents are the new SaaS — and YC W26 has shown the playbook

VoiceCommerce, VetMatchAI, AdAutopilot, AutoOps for Shopify and TalentAgent each take a single vertical or function and build an agent narrow enough to actually replace the human friction in it. YC W26's batch composition — Elyra (restaurants), Modern (IT/HR/Finance), Pollen (customer success), Corvera (CPG operations) — is the same pattern at scale. The category will be defined by vertical depth, not horizontal capability, because the unlock is operator-grade UX inside one workflow. Founders who pick a vertical and commit fully will out-execute generalist agent platforms.

The 11x failure mode is now mapped — and the next wave of agent startups is being designed around it

11x.ai's 70–80% customer churn and the fabricated-customer scandal turned "autonomous agents at $50K/year" from a category into a warning sign, while Clay's $100M ARR shows what worked instead: intelligence on top of human execution, not replacement of it. VirtualSDR Done Right, AdAutopilot and TalentAgent encode the lesson explicitly — outcome billing, hard guardrails, refund-on-failure, human checkpoints. The next 18 months of agent-startup design will be defined less by capability claims and more by the specific guardrails founders ship on day one. Buyers will reward sober pitches.

Vibe coding has exploded production-grade software — and exposed governance gaps that are themselves startup-shaped

Replit, Bolt, v0 and Lovable have dragged software creation into the hands of millions of non-technical operators (40% of recent YC batches used AI coding tools to build their MVPs), but they ship without SSO, role-based permissions or compliance baselines. VibeShield, CodeReviewAI and AIAuditTrail each fill a specific layer of the missing infrastructure — governance for SMB-built apps, business-language review for vibe-coded changes, cryptographic logging for agent actions. The category beneath the vibe-coding wave is now visible: the picks-and-shovels for a world where everyone ships code but very few can audit it.
